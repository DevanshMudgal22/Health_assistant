Good oral health prevents tooth decay, gum disease, bad breath, and tooth loss.
Brush your teeth at least twice a day using fluoride toothpaste for two minutes each time.
Floss daily to remove food particles and plaque from between the teeth and gumline.
Limit sugary and acidic foods and drinks as they erode tooth enamel and cause cavities.
Drink plenty of water especially fluoridated water to strengthen teeth and rinse food away.
Replace your toothbrush every three months or sooner if the bristles are frayed.
Avoid smoking and tobacco products as they cause gum disease and oral cancer.
Gum disease symptoms include swollen, bleeding gums, bad breath, and loose teeth.
Dental checkups every six months help detect problems early before they become serious.
Toothache, jaw pain, or swelling in the mouth requires prompt dental evaluation.
