Child health focuses on the physical, mental, and emotional well-being of children from birth to adolescence.
Vaccinations are essential to protect children from serious diseases like measles, polio, and whooping cough.
Breastfeeding for the first six months provides optimal nutrition and immunity for newborns.
Children need a balanced diet with fruits, vegetables, proteins, dairy, and whole grains for healthy growth.
Ensure children get adequate sleep as it is critical for brain development and immune function.
Limit screen time and encourage outdoor play and physical activity for healthy development.
Common childhood illnesses include fever, ear infections, colds, diarrhea, and skin rashes.
Maintain good hygiene habits like regular handwashing to prevent the spread of infections.
Monitor growth and developmental milestones through regular pediatric checkups.
Consult a pediatrician promptly if a child has high fever, difficulty breathing, persistent vomiting, or unusual behavior.
