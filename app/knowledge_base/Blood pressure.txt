Blood pressure is the force of blood pushing against the walls of your arteries.
High blood pressure (hypertension) is above 140/90 mmHg and often has no symptoms.
Low blood pressure (hypotension) can cause dizziness, fainting, and weakness.
Risk factors for hypertension include obesity, stress, excess salt intake, and smoking.
Headaches, shortness of breath, and nosebleeds may occur in severe hypertension.
Lifestyle changes like reducing salt, exercising regularly, and quitting smoking help manage blood pressure.
Doctors may prescribe medications such as ACE inhibitors or beta-blockers for hypertension.
Check blood pressure regularly, especially if you have a family history of heart disease.
Avoid excessive caffeine and alcohol as they can raise blood pressure.
Uncontrolled high blood pressure increases risk of stroke, heart attack, and kidney disease.
