Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition that causes inflammation and narrowing of the airways.
Common symptoms include wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, and persistent cough.
Triggers include dust, pollen, pet dander, smoke, cold air, exercise, and respiratory infections.
Avoid known triggers and keep living spaces clean and well-ventilated to reduce asthma episodes.
Inhalers are the most common treatment with reliever inhalers used during attacks and preventer inhalers used daily.
Always carry a reliever inhaler in case of a sudden asthma attack.
Monitor breathing using a peak flow meter to track asthma control over time.
Breathing exercises like diaphragmatic breathing can improve lung capacity and reduce symptoms.
Avoid smoking and second-hand smoke as they severely worsen asthma conditions.
Seek emergency medical help if breathing becomes very difficult, lips turn blue, or an inhaler provides no relief.
