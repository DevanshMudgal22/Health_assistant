Kidney stones are hard deposits of minerals and salts that form inside the kidneys.
Common symptoms include severe pain in the back or side, nausea, vomiting, and painful urination.
Urine may appear pink, red, or cloudy when kidney stones are present.
Drinking plenty of water (8-10 glasses daily) is the most effective way to prevent kidney stones.
Small stones often pass on their own with increased fluid intake and pain management.
Avoid excessive salt, animal protein, and oxalate-rich foods like spinach and nuts if prone to stones.
OTC pain relievers like ibuprofen can help manage discomfort during passing.
A doctor may prescribe medication to help relax the ureter and speed up stone passage.
Larger stones may require medical procedures like lithotripsy or surgical removal.
Seek immediate medical care if you have fever with pain, as it may indicate a kidney infection.
