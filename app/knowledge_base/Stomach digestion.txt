Digestive issues include bloating, acidity, constipation, diarrhea, and stomach cramps.
Acid reflux occurs when stomach acid flows back into the esophagus causing heartburn.
Eating slowly, chewing well, and avoiding overeating can prevent many digestive problems.
High-fiber foods like fruits, vegetables, and whole grains support healthy digestion.
Stay well hydrated as water helps move food through the digestive tract.
Avoid spicy, oily, and processed foods if you experience frequent stomach issues.
Probiotics found in yogurt and fermented foods can improve gut health.
Constipation can be relieved by increasing fiber intake, hydration, and physical activity.
Diarrhea lasting more than 2 days or accompanied by blood requires medical attention.
Persistent stomach pain, unexplained weight loss, or vomiting blood needs immediate medical care.
