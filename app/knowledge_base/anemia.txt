Anemia is a condition where the blood lacks enough healthy red blood cells to carry oxygen.
The most common type is iron-deficiency anemia caused by low iron levels in the body.
Symptoms include fatigue, weakness, pale skin, dizziness, shortness of breath, and cold hands.
Iron-rich foods include red meat, beans, lentils, spinach, tofu, and fortified cereals.
Vitamin C helps the body absorb iron better and should be consumed alongside iron-rich foods.
Folic acid and vitamin B12 deficiencies can also cause anemia and require dietary correction.
Avoid drinking tea or coffee with meals as they can inhibit iron absorption.
Iron supplements may be prescribed by a doctor for moderate to severe iron-deficiency anemia.
Women of reproductive age and pregnant women are at higher risk of developing anemia.
Consult a doctor for blood tests if you experience persistent fatigue or unexplained weakness.
