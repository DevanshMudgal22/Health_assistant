Pregnancy care involves maintaining the health of both the mother and the developing baby.
Regular prenatal checkups are essential to monitor fetal development and the mother's health.
Folic acid supplements before and during early pregnancy help prevent neural tube defects.
Eat a balanced diet rich in iron, calcium, protein, and vitamins to support fetal growth.
Avoid alcohol, smoking, and recreational drugs entirely during pregnancy as they harm the baby.
Stay physically active with gentle exercises like walking or prenatal yoga unless advised otherwise.
Common pregnancy symptoms include nausea, fatigue, back pain, and frequent urination.
Stay hydrated and get adequate rest as the body works harder during pregnancy.
Avoid certain foods like raw fish, unpasteurized dairy, and undercooked meat due to infection risk.
Seek immediate medical attention for severe abdominal pain, heavy bleeding, or reduced fetal movement.
