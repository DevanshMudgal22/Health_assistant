Sleep issues include insomnia, oversleeping, sleep apnea, and restless leg syndrome.
Adults generally need 7 to 9 hours of quality sleep per night for good health.
Poor sleep can lead to fatigue, mood changes, poor concentration, and weakened immunity.
Maintain a consistent sleep schedule by going to bed and waking at the same time daily.
Avoid screens, caffeine, and heavy meals at least one hour before bedtime.
Create a comfortable sleep environment that is cool, dark, and quiet.
Relaxation techniques like deep breathing or meditation can help improve sleep quality.
Regular physical activity during the day promotes better sleep at night.
Avoid alcohol as it disrupts sleep cycles even if it initially causes drowsiness.
Consult a doctor if you experience chronic insomnia, loud snoring, or excessive daytime sleepiness.
