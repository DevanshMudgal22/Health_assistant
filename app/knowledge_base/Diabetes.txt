Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body cannot properly regulate blood sugar levels.
Type 1 diabetes occurs when the body produces little or no insulin.
Type 2 diabetes is more common and occurs when the body doesn't use insulin effectively.
Common symptoms include frequent urination, excessive thirst, fatigue, and blurred vision.
Unexplained weight loss can also be an early sign of diabetes.
Management includes a balanced diet, regular exercise, and medication or insulin as prescribed.
Monitor blood sugar levels regularly as advised by your doctor.
Avoid sugary drinks, processed foods, and high-carbohydrate meals.
Diabetic patients should check their feet regularly for wounds or infections.
Seek medical help immediately if blood sugar levels are extremely high or low.
