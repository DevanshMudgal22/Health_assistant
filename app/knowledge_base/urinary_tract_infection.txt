A urinary tract infection (UTI) is a bacterial infection affecting the bladder, urethra, or kidneys.
Common symptoms include a burning sensation during urination, frequent urge to urinate, and cloudy or strong-smelling urine.
Lower abdominal pain or pressure is also a common symptom of a bladder infection.
Women are more prone to UTIs due to their shorter urethra compared to men.
Drink plenty of water to flush bacteria out of the urinary tract.
Urinate after sexual activity to reduce the risk of bacterial entry into the urethra.
Avoid holding urine for long periods as it allows bacteria to multiply in the bladder.
Wearing breathable cotton underwear and avoiding harsh soaps in the genital area helps prevent UTIs.
Antibiotics prescribed by a doctor are the standard treatment for confirmed UTIs.
Seek medical attention if symptoms include fever, chills, or back pain as it may indicate a kidney infection.
